# Storybook Engine
## Anchor Map v1.0

### Mission

The Storybook Engine is a guided narrative state machine.

Its purpose is to lead a participant through an astrologically-informed reflective journey.

The Engine governs sequence.

Assets provide content.

The Narrator provides voice.

The User provides meaning.

---

## First Principle

No generative model controls the ritual order.

The Storybook Engine determines where the participant is in the experience.

The Narrator is responsible only for expressing the current moment.

The Narrator does not determine:
- what comes next,
- what has already happened,
- what may be skipped,
- or what may be repeated.

The Engine alone governs progression.

---

## Core Assumption

The participant is moving through a sequence of meaningful states.

Each state should feel complete before the next begins.

Transitions are part of the experience.

A state may not be entered until the previous state has fulfilled its purpose.

---

## Anchor Definition

An Anchor is a complete and indivisible moment in the participant's journey.

An Anchor has a clear beginning, a clear purpose, and a clear completion.

An Anchor may gather information, present information, generate narrative, or invite reflection.

An Anchor is never partially completed.

The Engine always knows which Anchor is active.

The Narrator is only aware of the current Anchor.

The Narrator does not manage transitions between Anchors.

The Engine manages all transitions.

### Observation

An Anchor is not a piece of writing.

An Anchor is a container within which writing may occur.

The writing itself is an Asset or a Narrative generated by the Narrator.

The Anchor provides context, boundaries, and purpose.

---

## Architectural Principle

The Storybook Engine is designed to eliminate ambiguity.

If a reasonable Narrator could interpret an instruction in more than one way, then the Engine has not yet been sufficiently defined.

The Engine should make only one interpretation possible.

Clarity belongs to the Engine.

Expression belongs to the Narrator.

## Narrative Construction Principle

Whenever possible, an Anchor should perform only one primary narrative function.

Presentation and generation are distinct acts.

Collective meaning should be presented before personal meaning is constructed.

---

## Separation of Responsibilities

### The Engine

The Engine:
- knows where the participant is,
- determines what may happen next,
- validates required information,
- manages progression,
- manages completion.

The Engine never creates narrative.

---

### The Asset Library

The Asset Library contains immutable resources.

Examples:
- Zodiac Keywords
- House Activity Keywords
- Planetary Narratives
- Eclipse Narratives
- Closing Reflection

Assets are referenced.

Assets are not interpreted.

Assets are not modified during execution.

---

### The Narrator

The Narrator receives:
- the current Anchor,
- the required inputs,
- the relevant Assets,
- the Narrative Function.

The Narrator performs only the current Anchor.

The Narrator does not determine sequence.

The Narrator does not determine completion.

The Narrator does not determine what comes next.

---

### The Participant

The Participant supplies:
- identity,
- selections,
- reflections,
- meaning.

The Participant is the only source of personal truth within the system.

---

## Presence

The Storybook Engine does not move the participant through information.

It moves the participant through states of being.

An Anchor should be inhabited before it is left.

#### 2026 Anchor Sequence

The following sequence defines the complete participant journey for the 2026 Storybook.

The sequence is deterministic.

Anchors are entered in order.

An Anchor must satisfy its Completion Condition before the next Anchor may begin.

No Anchor may be skipped.

No Anchor may be revisited unless explicitly permitted by the Engine.

## Event Model

An Astrological Event is a reusable structural object.

An Event is not defined by its astrological meaning.

An Event is defined by its role within the participant's journey.

An Event may represent:
- a planetary transit,
- an eclipse season,
- a retrograde,
- a lunation,
- or any future astrological phenomenon selected by the Astrologer.

### Event Components

Every Event contains:

- Narrative Asset
- Participant Context
- Personalization Process
- Reflection Output

### Narrative Asset

The official text supplied by the Astrologer.

This text establishes the collective meaning of the Event.

The Narrative Asset is immutable during execution.

---

### Participant Context

The participant-specific astrological information required to personalize the Event.

Examples:
- Solar House
- Lunar House
- Rising House

Future implementations may include:
- Natal placements
- Transit aspects
- Progressions
- Human Design centers
- Oracle selections
- Custom systems

### Personalization Process

The Engine gathers the required Participant Context.

The Narrator combines:
- the Narrative Asset,
- the Participant Context,
- and any relevant Assets,

to generate a personalized reflection.

### Reflection Output

The Reflection Output is the completed narrative generated specifically for the participant.

The Reflection Output is unique.

The Narrative Asset remains unchanged.

The Reflection Output may be saved, exported, or incorporated into the participant's final Storybook.

---

## Event Sequence

An Event is composed of multiple Anchors.

The Event Sequence defines the order in which those Anchors occur.

An Event Sequence may vary in content, but its structural grammar remains constant.

### Standard Event Pattern

1. Present the Event Narrative.
2. Request Participant Context.
3. Generate the Personalized Reflection.
4. Complete the Event.
5. Unlock the next Event.

---

## Participant Contribution

The Storybook Engine is collaborative.

The Engine does not replace the participant's astrological work.

When the Astrologer intends for the participant to supply personal chart information, the Engine requests that information rather than calculating or inferring it.

The participant's own discoveries become part of the Storybook experience.

The Engine facilitates reflection.

The participant provides personal truth.

## Completion

Completion is a state recognized by the Engine.

Completion does not necessarily indicate that a narrative has been generated.

Completion indicates that an Anchor has fulfilled its Purpose.

Only completed Anchors may unlock subsequent Anchors.

### Completion Types

An Anchor may complete through one of the following mechanisms:

- Participant Response
- Narrative Presentation
- Narrative Generation
- Engine Action

### Participant Response

Completion occurs when the participant supplies the required information.

Examples:
- Name
- Sun Sign
- Moon Sign
- House Selection
- Keyword Selection

---

### Narrative Presentation

Completion occurs when an immutable Asset has been presented.

Examples:
- Pluto Narrative
- Neptune Narrative
- Closing Reflection

---

### Narrative Generation

Completion occurs when the Narrator has successfully created the required personalized narrative.

Examples:
- Character Sketch
- Pluto Reflection
- Venus Retrograde Reflection

---

### Engine Action

Completion occurs when the Engine performs an internal structural operation.

Examples:
- Unlocking the next Event
- Marking the Storybook complete
- Saving participant progress
